Companion to the **WHEC** poster *"Chillerless Fast-Fill for Heavy-Duty Hydrogen
Vehicles: PCM Thermal Buffering Eliminates Pre-Cooling Requirements"* — **B. Chiramel, HPCL**.

Eliminate the −40 °C station chiller by moving thermal management **onboard**: a conductive
tank liner plus a phase-change-material (PCM) buffer keeps a 700-bar, 10-minute, ambient fill
under the 85 °C structural ceiling — no pre-cooling required.

### What you can do in the simulator

| Mode | |
|---|---|
| **▶ Story walkthrough** | A guided 7-step tour: standard tank fails → today's chiller → fix the liner → add the PCM sponge → it passes → the limits → the payoff. |
| **⚙ Sandbox** | Drag the controls — fill temperature, refuelling time, liner material & conductivity, PCM on/off, material and mass — and watch the safety verdict flip live. |
| **📜 Where we came from** | The design history: pressure-vessel Types I–IV, the liner's three conductivity eras, PCM's two failures and one breakthrough, and the refuelling-protocol evolution. |

Every thermal curve is **pre-computed by the real coupled gas–wall engine** (`fastfill_350.py`,
real-gas H₂); the page just looks values up, so it is instant and runs entirely in your browser.
Each value is tagged **[EXT]** (public source) or **[CALC]** (real engine).
